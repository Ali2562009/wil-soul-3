The person behind the words

The Wild
Soul

A free spirit who left the noise of the world, walked into nature, and never fully came back — and has no regrets about it.

"I didn't go looking for peace. I just stopped running from silence — and peace was already there."

My story

I was not always this person. There was a time when I lived like everyone else — fast, distracted, filling every moment with noise because silence felt dangerous. I did things I'm not proud of. I went places I shouldn't have gone. I called it freedom but it was the opposite of free.

The turn didn't come dramatically. No single moment, no thunderbolt. It came slowly, the way dawn comes — you don't see it happening but suddenly everything is different. I started going outside more. Not for exercise, not for a goal. Just to be somewhere that wasn't a screen or a room or a conversation I didn't want to be having.

"Nature doesn't ask anything of you. It just receives you. That was what I needed most."

I'm a Muslim. I was raised Muslim but for years I wore it lightly — more habit than heart. Coming back to it was not about guilt or fear. It was about recognition. Sitting outside at Fajr time, watching the sky go from black to deep blue to gold, I felt something I couldn't name. Later I realized it was just — gratitude. Real gratitude, not the performed kind.

I started writing things down. Not for anyone. Just because the moments felt too good to lose. A funny thing that happened with a goat. Something a farmer said about patience. A feeling I had standing in cold water. One by one the notes became letters. The letters became this website.

This is not a blog. It's not a brand. It's just a person — imperfect, still learning, occasionally ridiculous — writing honestly about a life lived outside the noise. If something here lands for you, I'm glad. If it makes you laugh or cry or think about something differently, then it did exactly what it was supposed to do.

What I actually believe

Not rules. Just things I've found to be true.

I

Silence is not empty

We are afraid of quiet. We fill it with music, phones, conversation. But silence is where the most honest thoughts live. Learn to sit in it and it will teach you more than any book.

II

God is easier to find outside

I'm not saying don't go to the mosque. I'm saying the mosque and the mountain are not opposites. The same presence I feel in sujood I feel standing next to a waterfall. Both are real.

III

A life without laughter is not a religious life

The Prophet ﷺ laughed. He played. He was gentle and funny and warm. Anyone who tells you faith means being serious and heavy all the time has misread the whole thing.

IV

You don't need more. You need less.

Every time I've carried less — fewer things, fewer obligations, fewer opinions about who I should be — I've been happier. The wild taught me this. Everything in nature takes only what it needs.

Things that make me feel alive

01
Cold water in the morning
River, sea, rain — doesn't matter. Cold water wakes something up that coffee never could.
02
Fajr outside
The world before sunrise belongs to a different kind of quiet. It's the best time there is.
03
Conversations with strangers
Old farmers. Tired travelers. Children who say what adults are too polite to say. They're all teachers.
04
Getting lost on purpose
No map, no plan. Following a path because it looks interesting. This is how I find the best places.
05
Tea made slowly
Not quickly. Not from a bag. Slowly, carefully, outside if possible. The making is the point.
06
Writing by hand
The pen slows you down. It makes you choose your words. Everything written by hand feels more honest.
"I am still becoming. I think that's the point."
