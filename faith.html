Quiet Faith

This is not a place of rules and lectures. It's a place where faith shows up the way it actually does — in the middle of a forest, in a funny moment, in silence before dawn.

أَلَا بِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ تَطْمَئِنُّ الْقُلُوبُ
"Verily, in the remembrance of God do hearts find rest."
Quran · Surah Ar-Ra'd · 13:28
Notes on faith from the wild
Note 01

Faith is not something you perform. It's something you return to.

I spent years performing. Saying the right words, making the right movements, when people were watching. What I found outside — in the quiet places, away from any audience — was that real faith has nothing to do with anyone else seeing it. It's between you and the One who already knows everything anyway.

Note 02

The Prophet ﷺ loved nature. I think about this a lot.

He noticed things. The smell of rain. The behavior of birds. He spoke about trees with tenderness. He made salah on the earth itself. There is a whole tradition of noticing the natural world as an act of worship — and somehow we forgot it, replaced it with fluorescent lighting and crowds. I'm trying to remember it.

Note 03

Tawbah is not a door closing. It is a window opening.

I always imagined returning to God as something heavy and dramatic — a long list of sins, a judge, a sentence. What I found was almost embarrassingly simple. A stream. A verse I knew as a child. A cry I'd been holding for years. And then — slowly — something that felt like being known, forgiven, and welcomed back. The word tawbah means to turn. Not to earn, not to explain. Just to turn.

Note 04

Laughter is sunnah. I have sources.

He ﷺ laughed until his back teeth showed. He played. He raced with Aisha. He was warm, funny, gentle, and full of life. Anyone who tells you a good Muslim must be grave and heavy all the time has simply not read the seerah carefully enough. Joy is not a distraction from faith. It is sometimes the evidence of it.

What faith actually looks like, for me

✦
Praying Fajr outside while the sky goes from black to blue to gold. This is the best prayer of the day and I will die on this hill.
✦
Saying alhamdulillah and actually meaning it, not just as a reflex. Sometimes it takes standing next to something beautiful to mean it properly.
✦
Laughing at something absurd and feeling grateful that God made humor. The goat incident comes to mind.
✦
Sitting in silence long enough to hear whatever was underneath all the noise. It's almost always something gentle.
✦
Returning after being away — not perfectly, not all at once — just turning back. Tawbah is a practice, not a single event.
✦
Noticing. A bird, a cloud, a river, a color in the sky. Everything is a sign for those paying attention. وَفِي الْأَرْضِ آيَاتٌ
"I am not a scholar. I'm just a person who went outside and found that God was already there."
